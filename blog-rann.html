कच्छ नहीं देखा तो कुछ नहीं देखा

Translation If you havent seen Kutch then you havent seen anything. is the slogan for Kutch tourism. How apt? I'll let you decide your self towards the end.

The Rann of Kutch is a large area of salt marshes that span the border between India and Pakistan. It is located mostly in Gujarat, India and in some parts of Sindh, Pakistan.

Rann Utsav is a festival of Kutch. It is a Carnival of Music, Dance, nature beauty of White Rann. An area near the desert is secured to establish the tent city to facilate the celebration.



A glimse of the tent city was enough to excite me about Rann Utsav, the colorful panoramic sky made the experience magical. The sky is clear of light pollution, this makes it ideal for star gazing.
Rann being a salt desert limits the mode of travel to bumpy camel rides, one can still opt for buses but the fun isn't tantamount.



Puppet shows are absolutely famous at Rann. One can find copious amount of puppet show at Rann each narrating a different story.


The flee market compromises of traditional gujrati outfit and amazing souvenir. Prices tend to be inflated, bargaining is the key.

How to plan for Rann?

The booking are centralized under tent city which makes the planning very convenient, there are many vendors for the same, the one we chose was RannUtsav.com
After the booking the only step is to arrive at Bhuj Railway station. Bhuj is a small town - 60kms from Rann. They have a small pitstop for guests arriving to the festival from where there are regular buses to orchestrate the transfer to the tent city. The travel itenary can be found here



Tips
>Travelling during full moon is the best.
>Do carry ample cash, necessities and power adaptor as tent city is far off general market.
